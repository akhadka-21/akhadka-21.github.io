My work builds on my expertise in causal inference, data science, and survey science. I collaborate closely with XFN spanning product, engineering, data science, and user experience research teams. More recently, I have been leveraging AI tooling to re-think the research pipeline and build research-relevant products.

Before Meta, I was a Postdoctoral Scholar at the University of California San Francisco. I received my PhD in Population Health Sciences from Harvard University where I specialized in health economics and epidemiology. I have a Masters in Global Health and Population from the Harvard T. H. Chan School of Public Health and a Bachelors in Mathematics and Political Science from Williams College.  

Prior to graduate school, I worked as an economic researcher and research manager at the Economic Policy Research Institute in Cape Town, South Africa.
