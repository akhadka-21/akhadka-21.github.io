I am a Research Scientist in the Demography and Survey Science org at Meta, where I design and analyze on-platform experiments and large-scale surveys to improve ads experiences, uncover ecosystem-level behavioral dynamics, and inform key monetization decisions using log-scale data. 

My work builds on my expertise in causal inference, data science, and survey science. I collaborate closely with XFN spanning product, engineering, data science, and user experience research teams. More recently, I have been leveraging AI tooling to re-think the research and data science workflows and to build research-relevant products.

Before Meta, I was a Postdoctoral Scholar at the University of California San Francisco. I received my Ph.D. in Population Health Sciences from Harvard University where I specialized in health economics, econometrics methods, and epidemiologic causal inference methods. I have a M.Sc. in Global Health and Population from the Harvard T. H. Chan School of Public Health and a B.A. in Political Science and Mathematics from Williams College.  

Prior to graduate school, I worked as an economic researcher at the Economic Policy Research Institute in Cape Town, South Africa.
